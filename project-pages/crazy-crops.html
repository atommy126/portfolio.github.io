Crazy Crops

Summary

Crazy crops is a farming-themed tabletop card game. There are four types of cards: Crop Cards, Curse Cards, Blessing Cards, and Weather Cards. One Crop Card can be planted per turn, and each player can only have a total of three planted at once. Crop Cards start at age 1, and all increase in age by 1 at the start of their owner's turn. Once they reach their maturity age, they can be harvested for points or other effects. Players may play as many Blessing and Curse Cards as they wish to per turn. Blessing Cards provide beneficial effects, and Curse Cards cause negative effects. Weather Cards cause effects that impact all players, and when one is drawn, it comes into play immediately.

Roles & Responsibilities

I came up with the concept for the game, as well as game mechanics, card design, and card text. I then led a team of four in the production of the game, physical cards, and tabletop simulator port. We also kept detailed design documentation on our design process and decisions, which I made sure was regularly updated throughout the game's development.

Playtesting

A major point during development was the first playtest. For this playtest, we used physical card prototypes, which were just index cards with handwritten card text and sketches. I was supervising the playtest and taking notes, and the game played out smoothly for its first time being played with players outside of the development team. However, there were a number of rules clarifications that had to be made and questions on card text wording. After the playtest, I made rules adjustments based on my notes and the data obtained from a playtest survey we created. I also took notes during our other playtests, which helped a ton when balancing the game.

Card Design - Wording

To keep the game consistent, I created a standard for the card text wording. I wanted to keep card text as short and simple as possible, while still allowing for complex card combinations. I also wanted to avoid using too many keywords, as they tend to appear intimidating to players who are unfamiliar with the game. All keywords used are explained within the rules document. Other keyword-like card properties are explained on the card, which makes the game much easier for newer players. I also balanced cards around a point per turn system, which helped me design both weaker and stronger cards.

Card Design - Interaction

Cards were also designed to increase interaction between players. For example, the Reap Curse Card can be used to harvest any fully grown Crop Card on the board. Harvesting on your own turn is free, so there is little point to playing it on your own cards. However, opponents are likely to harvest their Crop Cards as soon as they are fully grown, so one of the best uses of the Reap Card is to play it with a Blessing Card, like Fertilizer. Fertilizer increases the age of any Crop Card, so you can use it to grow an opponent's crop, then play Reap and steal the rewards from them.

Card Design - Visuals

I also created a standardized design for all of the cards. I first decided on a basic layout and color scheme. Crazy Crops' target audience included younger players, and was likely to be played with family and friends. This led me to choose pastel springtime colors for the cards, with colors being symbolic for each type of card. I also chose Noto Serif as the font for the cards, as the serifs improved card readability and created a serious, yet still inviting tone. The cards have a large space for the image to go, which makes cards more visually distinctive, while still leaving room for a sizable area for rules text.
